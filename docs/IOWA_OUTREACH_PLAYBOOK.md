## Quick Onboarding: Demo Account Flow

### Current Process (Manual)

1. **You create their account** via Super Admin panel
   - Go to **Admin > People Management**
   - Create user with their .edu email
   - Assign role: `class_admin`

2. **You create a Demo Organization**
   - Go to **Admin > Organizations**
   - Create: "[School Name] - Demo"
   - Generate team code for their demo class
   - Add them as class admin

3. **They log in and explore sandbox**
   - They visit futureworkacademy.com
   - "Sign in with Replit" (uses their email)
   - They see their Demo organization
   - Click **"Enter Preview Mode"** to experience student view

4. **You add test students (optional)**
   - Create 2-3 fake students for their demo org
   - Allows them to see leaderboard, team dynamics

## Explainer Script: Academic Integrity Focus

*Use this for phone calls, Zoom demos, or as voiceover for the explainer video.*

---

### Opening (30 seconds)

"The Future of Work is an 8-week business simulation where your students become executives at a mid-sized manufacturing company facing a critical decision: how to adopt AI while managing workforce anxiety, union relations, and stakeholder expectations.

Unlike case studies, students make decisions and see consequences unfold. Unlike other simulations, every response is evaluated by AI with transparent rubric scoring."

---

### Academic Integrity (45 seconds)

"Let me address the elephant in the room: AI and academic integrity.

We've designed this differently. Students can't just paste their response into ChatGPT because:

1. **Context-specific prompts** - Each week's decision is based on unique briefing materials they just read. Generic AI responses miss the nuances.

2. **Transparent rubric scoring** - We show students exactly how they're graded: Strategic Thinking, Stakeholder Awareness, Risk Assessment, and Communication Quality. They see the rubric before they write.

3. **Comparative visibility** - After deadline, students see anonymized top responses. If someone's response doesn't match their class participation, it's obvious.

4. **The goal is learning, not gotchas** - We want students to think like executives, not avoid AI. The simulation rewards synthesis and judgment, not information retrieval."

---

### Key Differentiators (45 seconds)

"Three things make this different from other simulations:

**First: Real pressure, real consequences.** Students make resource allocation decisions - dollars to AI, reskilling, lobbying, union relations. Each week, they deal with the fallout of their choices.

**Second: Dual scoring.** Financial performance AND cultural health. Students learn that sustainable leadership means both. Cut corners on culture? Watch your workforce morale tank.

**Third: Rich narrative.** 17 AI-generated stakeholder characters - the worried CFO, the skeptical union rep, the ambitious CTO. Students receive triggered voicemails when they cross thresholds. It feels real."

---

### The Ask (30 seconds)

"I've set up a sandbox account for you. You can log in, experience the student view, make some decisions, and see how the AI feedback works.

If it's a fit for [course name], we can discuss piloting it this [semester]. Typical cohort pricing is $75-100 per student for 15-25 students.

What questions do you have?"

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Students will just use ChatGPT" | See Academic Integrity section above. The simulation rewards synthesis, not retrieval. Plus, we show the rubric upfront - students know exactly what earns points. |
| "We already use [other simulation]" | Most simulations are quantitative only. We score financial AND cultural health. And our AI-powered feedback is unique. Would you like to see a side-by-side comparison? |
| "It's too expensive" | At $75-100/student, it's comparable to textbook supplements. For Iowa institutions, this may qualify as a 260F training expense if partnered through a community college. |
| "I need to see it first" | Absolutely. I'll set you up with a demo account today. You can explore at your own pace. |
| "My students won't engage" | The triggered voicemails and character ecosystem create emotional stakes. Students tell us it feels like a real job. |
