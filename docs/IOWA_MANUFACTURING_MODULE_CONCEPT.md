## 1. Module Overview

### Title Options
- "Heartland 4.0: Navigating Manufacturing's Digital Transformation"
- "Prairie Industries: AI, Automation, and the Iowa Workforce"
- "Midwest Manufacturing 2030: The Automation Decision"

### Elevator Pitch

Step into the role of VP of Operations at **Prairie Precision Manufacturing**, a mid-sized Iowa company producing agricultural equipment components. Over 8 weeks, navigate the decision to invest in AI-powered quality inspection and robotic assembly while managing a multigenerational workforce, union negotiations, and community expectations. Balance profitability with your responsibility to the town that depends on your factory.

### Target Audience

| Audience | Use Case |
|----------|----------|
| Manufacturing supervisors/managers | 260F workforce development |
| Community college students | Manufacturing technology programs |
| MBA/Executive Education | Regional business leadership |
| Economic development professionals | Understanding manufacturer challenges |

## 2. Company Profile: Prairie Precision Manufacturing

### Company Background

- **Founded**: 1962 by the Andersen family
- **Location**: Fictional "Millbrook, Iowa" (composite of Iowa manufacturing towns)
- **Employees**: 340 (down from 420 peak in 2015)
- **Products**: Precision components for agricultural equipment (John Deere, AGCO, CNH)
- **Annual Revenue**: $48 million
- **Ownership**: Third-generation family-owned, considering private equity partnership

### Current Situation (Week 1)

- **Challenge**: Major customer (representing 35% of revenue) demands 15% cost reduction within 18 months or will shift to Mexican supplier
- **Opportunity**: State Manufacturing 4.0 grant available for automation investment
- **Constraint**: UAW Local 847 contract expires in 8 months; workforce skeptical of automation
- **Community Context**: Millbrook population 4,200; factory is largest employer

### Key Metrics (Starting Position)

| Metric | Starting Value | Target Range |
|--------|----------------|--------------|
| Revenue | $48M | Maintain or grow |
| Operating Margin | 6.2% | Improve to 8%+ |
| Workforce | 340 | Maintain 300+ |
| Employee Retention | 78% | Improve to 85%+ |
| Union Relations | Neutral (50) | Maintain 40+ |
| Community Trust | Strong (72) | Maintain 60+ |
| Quality (Defect Rate) | 2.3% | Reduce to <1.5% |

## 4. Weekly Scenario Structure

### Week 1: The Ultimatum
**Intelligence Briefing**: Customer cost reduction demand; competitive pressure from Mexico and automation trends in manufacturing

**Key Decision**: How do you respond to the 15% cost reduction demand?
- Option A: Promise to meet demand through automation (aggressive)
- Option B: Propose phased cost reduction with partial automation (moderate)
- Option C: Push back and emphasize quality/reliability value (defensive)
- Option D: Begin exploring alternative customers to reduce dependence (diversify)

**Essay Prompt**: "Draft a memo to Carl Andersen outlining your recommended response to the customer ultimatum and the workforce implications."

---

### Week 2: The Investment Decision
**Intelligence Briefing**: Manufacturing 4.0 grant details; automation vendor proposals; ROI analysis

**Key Decision**: What level of automation investment do you pursue?
- Option A: Full automation package ($2.4M) - Quality inspection + robotic assembly
- Option B: Phased approach ($1.2M Year 1) - Quality inspection first
- Option C: Minimal investment ($400K) - Upgrade existing equipment
- Option D: Delay decision pending union contract resolution

**Resource Allocation**: Split budget between equipment, training, communication, and contingency

**Essay Prompt**: "Write a justification memo for your investment recommendation, addressing both financial returns and workforce impact."

---

### Week 3: The Union Challenge
**Intelligence Briefing**: UAW Local 847 history; comparable automation negotiations; legal considerations

**Key Decision**: How do you approach the union about automation plans?
- Option A: Negotiate job guarantees in exchange for automation acceptance
- Option B: Present automation as opportunity with retraining commitment
- Option C: Proceed with minimal union engagement (management rights)
- Option D: Propose joint labor-management automation committee

**Essay Prompt**: "Draft talking points for your first meeting with Dale Henrickson (UAW President) about the automation investment."

---

### Week 4: The Workforce Transition Plan
**Intelligence Briefing**: Retraining options; community college partnership possibilities; severance considerations

**Key Decision**: How do you structure workforce transition?
- Option A: Retrain all affected workers for new roles
- Option B: Offer early retirement incentives + retrain remaining workers
- Option C: Combination of retraining, attrition, and limited layoffs
- Option D: Partner with community college for certification program

**Resource Allocation**: Training budget, severance reserves, communication investment

**Essay Prompt**: "Design a workforce transition plan that addresses both business needs and employee concerns."

---

### Week 5: The Community Response
**Global Event**: Local newspaper runs story on automation investment; community reaction

**Intelligence Briefing**: Community stakeholder mapping; economic impact studies; messaging strategies

**Key Decision**: How do you manage community relations?
- Option A: Proactive community town hall explaining plans
- Option B: Low-profile approach, focus on positive job creation message
- Option C: Partner with mayor on workforce development announcement
- Option D: Let results speak for themselves, avoid public statements

**Essay Prompt**: "Draft a statement for the local newspaper addressing community concerns about the automation investment."

---

### Week 6: The Implementation Challenge
**Intelligence Briefing**: Change management research; implementation case studies; resistance patterns

**Key Decision**: How do you handle implementation resistance?
- Option A: Identify and empower internal automation champions
- Option B: Bring in external change management consultants
- Option C: Create pilot program with volunteer team
- Option D: Top-down implementation with clear timeline

**Essay Prompt**: "Analyze the root causes of resistance you're encountering and propose specific interventions."

---

### Week 7: The Crisis
**Global Event**: Implementation setback (equipment malfunction, training delay, or worker injury—randomized)

**Intelligence Briefing**: Crisis communication best practices; recovery strategies; stakeholder management

**Key Decision**: How do you respond to the crisis?
- Option A: Pause implementation for full review
- Option B: Address specific issue while maintaining timeline
- Option C: Accelerate to get past "valley of despair"
- Option D: Bring in third-party expertise for assessment

**Essay Prompt**: "Write an internal communication to all employees addressing the current situation and path forward."

---

### Week 8: The New Normal
**Intelligence Briefing**: Results to date; customer feedback; workforce metrics; lessons learned

**Key Decision**: What is your long-term automation strategy?
- Option A: Expand automation to additional production lines
- Option B: Consolidate current gains before further investment
- Option C: Pivot to workforce development as competitive advantage
- Option D: Diversify customer base to reduce pressure

**Final Essay**: "Reflecting on your 8-week journey, what did you learn about managing technology transitions in manufacturing? What would you do differently?"
