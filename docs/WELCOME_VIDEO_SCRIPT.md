# Welcome Video Script

> **For:** Student-facing welcome walkthrough video  
> **Format:** Doug on camera for intro/outro, screen recording for middle sections  
> **Total Length:** ~60 seconds  
> **File Location:** `client/public/videos/welcome-intro.mp4`

---

## Opening — You on Camera (10 sec)

"Welcome to Future Work Academy. I'm Doug, and over the next few weeks, you're going to run a company through one of the biggest transformations in business today — the adoption of AI."

---

## Transition to Screen Recording — Dashboard (15 sec)

"This is your command center. Each week, you'll get an intelligence briefing about what's happening at Apex Manufacturing — real scenarios, real stakes. Your job is to make decisions that balance the bottom line with the people who make the company work."

---

## Quick Flash of Decisions Page (10 sec)

"You'll face tough calls — budget trade-offs, employee pushback, technology risks. There's no single right answer, but your reasoning matters. Every essay response is scored on both financial impact and cultural health."

---

## Quick Flash of Characters/People Page (10 sec)

"You're not doing this alone. Seventeen characters — from the CFO to the union organizer — will react to your choices. And if you get stuck, you've got nine advisors you can call for strategic guidance."

---

## Flash Leaderboard (5 sec)

"Your team's performance is tracked on a live leaderboard. It's competitive, but the real goal is learning how to lead through complexity."

---

## Closing — You on Camera (10 sec)

"Check your Student Guide for the full details, and when you're ready — dive in. Good luck."

---

## Production Notes

- Keep screen recordings tight — show just enough UI to orient the viewer
- Dashboard shot should show the weekly briefing panel prominently
- Characters page shot should pan across a few headshots to convey the cast
- Leaderboard flash can be quick, just enough to see team rankings
- Warm, confident tone throughout — encouraging, not lecturing
