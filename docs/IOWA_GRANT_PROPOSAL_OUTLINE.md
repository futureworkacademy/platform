# Iowa Workforce Development Grant Proposal Outline
## Future Work Academy - Manufacturing Workforce Training

**Target Programs:** 260F Iowa Jobs Training Program, Manufacturing 4.0 Technology Investment Grant  
**Prepared by:** Future Work Academy  
**Date:** January 2026

---

## Executive Summary

Future Work Academy (FWA) is an immersive business simulation platform that prepares manufacturing supervisors, managers, and emerging leaders to navigate AI adoption, workforce transformation, and stakeholder management decisions. This proposal outlines how FWA can serve as a qualified training expense under Iowa's 260F Iowa Jobs Training Program and/or the Manufacturing 4.0 Technology Investment Grant to upskill Iowa's manufacturing workforce for Industry 4.0 challenges.

---

## 1. Problem Statement

Iowa's manufacturing sector faces unprecedented workforce challenges:

- **AI/Automation Transition**: $32B manufacturing GDP goal by 2026 requires Industry 4.0 adoption
- **Leadership Skills Gap**: Front-line supervisors lack training in managing technology transitions
- **Workforce Anxiety**: Employees fear displacement; managers struggle to communicate change effectively
- **Union Relations**: Labor-management dynamics complicate automation decisions
- **Retention Risk**: Poor transition management leads to turnover and lost institutional knowledge

**Traditional training approaches fail** because:
- Static case studies don't capture real-time decision complexity
- Classroom instruction lacks consequences for poor decisions
- One-time workshops don't build lasting "decision-making muscle"

---

## 2. Proposed Solution

### Future Work Academy Simulation Platform

An 8-week immersive business simulation where participants:

1. **Step into executive roles** at a fictional manufacturing company navigating AI adoption
2. **Make weekly strategic decisions** on automation investment, workforce reskilling, union negotiations, and stakeholder communication
3. **Experience consequences** through dynamic scoring (Financial Health + Cultural Health metrics)
4. **Receive AI-powered feedback** on decision rationale and stakeholder consideration
5. **Compete in cohorts** via live leaderboards, fostering engagement and peer learning

### Iowa Manufacturing Module (Proposed)

A custom scenario tailored to Iowa/Midwest manufacturing realities:
- Agricultural equipment and food processing contexts
- Rural workforce dynamics and community impact
- Heartland union culture and labor relations
- Supply chain reshoring decisions
- State/regional incentive considerations

## 5. 260F Eligibility Alignment

### Eligible Training Activities (Iowa Code 260F)

| Requirement | FWA Alignment |
|-------------|---------------|
| Training that enhances employee performance | Decision-making skills for technology transitions |
| Provided through community college partnership | Platform license through community college business training division |
| Reimbursable training costs | Per-seat licensing model, clear cost documentation |
| Measurable training outcomes | Built-in assessment, scoring, and completion tracking |

### Proposed Partnership Structure

1. **Iowa Community College** serves as training provider/administrator
2. **Future Work Academy** provides platform access and Iowa-specific content
3. **Participating Manufacturers** enroll supervisors/managers as participants
4. **260F Funding** covers platform licensing as qualified training expense

## Appendix: About Future Work Academy

Future Work Academy is an AI-powered business simulation platform designed for graduate business education and workforce development. The platform features:

- **Research-Backed Pedagogy**: Addresses the "Relevance Gap" in management education (Bartunek & Rynes, 2014)

- Proven engagement metrics and completion rates
